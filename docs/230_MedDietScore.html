MedDiet Score - Baseline

We will use Mediterranean Diet Score (MedDietScore) to assess whether participant’s adherence to mediterranean diet (MD) shows any association with microbiome composition or derived parameters. MedDietScore is an integer score ranging from 0 to 14, obtained from a simplified dietary questionnaire (PREDIMED). A medDietScore of 14 indicates maximal adherence to MD while 0 indicates no adherence at all.

Alpha Diversity - Baseline

We will check, at baseline, if MedDietScore shows any association with Alpha diversity measures (Chao1, Shannon, PD). Results indicate no correlation between any of the alpha-diversity parameters and MedDietScore.

MDScore-Genus correlation

Apparently, there is a significant correlation between NMDS1 and MedDiet Score. Note, that according to baseline exploration, NMDS1 loads were clearly dominated by Bacteroides presence. In addition we will examine also other genus within this dataset. We will select those that are present in at least 20% of the samples. When we examine association between this genus and other and MedDiet Score we detect:

  • Weak but significant correlations between MDScore and Lachnospira and Ruminococcus Genera
  • Weak but significant inverse correlations between MDScore and Bifidobacterium and Oscillibacter genera.

Interestingly, Lachnospira genus showed to be enriched after nutrititional intervention in the Intervention Group(C01) but not in the control group(C02). Lachnospiraceae family species are butyrate producers, which specifically habit mammal digestive tract. Their role in human health is [controverted] (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7232163/)

Lachnospira was also found to be enriched in higher MD-adherent healthy dults. Lachnospira spp also increased in lean individuals upon physical exercising program, linked to an increase in the detection of butyrate and other SCFAs in feces. Other studies have linked Lachnospira to physical activity or active lifestyle.

Lachnospira has also been linked to the consumption of high-fiber diet in two independent populations and a randomized clinical study in healthy adults shifting from whole grains to refined ones

MD Changes vs Alpha diversity

We want to see if there is any relationship between the magnitude of change in MD adherence (reported through MDScore) and the changes in alpha-diversity. For that we will calculate the relative changes in alpha diversity (Shannon only) for each of the participants, defined as either the (ShannonMonth6 - ShannonBL) difference or the (ShannonMonth6 - ShannonBL)/(ShannonMonth6 + ShannonBL) relative difference and check statistical association with changes in MDScore. Results indicated that there is no significant relationship.

Stratified Analysis

In order to further study whether increased MD-Adherence was related to the increase in several bacterial genera we souhgt to stratify individuals by the increase on MDscore along the study. For this we extracted the 10% individuals with the highest increased adherence to MD, disregarding the center where they were allocated to, in order to characterize the changes of their microbiome after the intervention time window.

Importantly, observed difference in the upper DeltaMDScore quartile, were not observed in the CG upper DeltaMDScore quartile, but were observed in the IG lower DeltaMDSCore (those with reduced adherence to MD in Month6). This may indicate that Increases in butyrate producing genus are related to the intervention itself, not to the changes in adherence to MD, as assessed by MDScore. While, presumably, multifactor intervention has not had the same effect on all individuals on IG, some individuals in the CG may have improved their lifestyle despite receiving only a subgroup of intervention measures.

MDScore only assesses the adherence to MD. While diet is a key component of the multifactorial intervention, other components may have had some effect on the gut microbiome composition. Interestingly, the multifactorial intervention also consisted on counseling to improve physical activity. Physical Activity was assessed at BL and Month6 using IPAQ score but can also be ascertained by changes in BMI

DeltaMDScore-Genus

An independent way to try to find changes associated to the mediterranean intervention is to focus on the intervention group, and calculate, for every patient, the change in the MDScore, that we can calculate either as absolute or relative value, and try to find associations with bacterial genus increases or decreases in their relative abundance. That is, the change in % of each genus between Month6 and BL samples.
